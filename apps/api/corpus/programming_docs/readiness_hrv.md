# Readiness Metrics: HRV, RHR, and Sleep

## Heart Rate Variability (HRV)

HRV measures the variation in time between heartbeats (R-R intervals). Higher HRV generally indicates better recovery and parasympathetic dominance. Lower HRV suggests sympathetic activation (stress, fatigue, illness).

### HRV Types

- **RMSSD**: Root Mean Square of Successive Differences — most commonly used in wearables (Oura). More sensitive to short-term autonomic changes. Typical range: 20-80ms.
- **SDNN**: Standard Deviation of NN intervals — Apple Watch reports this from Breathe/mindfulness sessions. Represents overall HRV. Typical range: 30-100ms.
- **Note**: RMSSD and SDNN are not interchangeable. Track each independently against its own baseline.

### Using HRV for Training Decisions

Compare today's HRV against your 28-day rolling mean (± 1 SD):

- **> +1 SD above baseline**: Green light — high readiness. Good day for intense training.
- **Within 1 SD**: Yellow — normal. Proceed with planned training.
- **1–2 SD below baseline**: Orange — reduced readiness. Consider dropping intensity 10-15%.
- **> 2 SD below baseline**: Red — significant suppression. Active recovery only.

### HRV Confounders

- Alcohol: reduces HRV significantly 1-2 days after consumption
- Poor sleep: HRV drops proportionally to sleep quality
- Intense training (normal): HRV typically drops day-after heavy session, recovers day 2-3
- Illness: sustained HRV suppression (3+ days) — a key early warning sign

## Resting Heart Rate (RHR)

RHR is most accurate when measured immediately upon waking. Elevated RHR (>5 bpm above baseline) is an indicator of incomplete recovery.

### RHR Norms

- Well-trained endurance athletes: 40-50 bpm
- Recreational CrossFit athletes: 50-65 bpm
- Elevated concern threshold: >10% above personal baseline

## Sleep Quality

Sleep is the most powerful recovery intervention available. Targets:

- **Duration**: 7-9 hours for performance athletes
- **REM sleep**: 20-25% of total sleep — critical for skill consolidation and mood
- **Deep sleep**: 15-20% of total sleep — critical for tissue repair and HGH release
- **Consistency**: Regular sleep/wake times are more important than duration alone

### Oura Sleep Score

0-100 composite score incorporating: total sleep, efficiency, restfulness, REM %, deep %, timing, latency. Score of 85+ = optimal for training. Score < 70 = consider reducing planned intensity.
