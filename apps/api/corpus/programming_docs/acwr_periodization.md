# Training Load Management: ACWR and Periodization

## Acute:Chronic Workload Ratio (ACWR)

ACWR = Acute Load (7-day rolling average) ÷ Chronic Load (28-day rolling average)

The ACWR is a key metric for injury risk monitoring and training load prescription.

### Interpretation

- **< 0.8**: Undertraining zone — athlete is doing significantly less than chronic load. Risk of detraining.
- **0.8–1.3**: Sweet spot (optimal training zone) — manageable load, low injury risk.
- **1.3–1.5**: Caution zone — elevated injury risk, monitor recovery markers.
- **> 1.5**: Danger zone — substantially increased injury risk (2–4x baseline). Back off.

### Calculation for CrossFit

Use session RPE × duration in minutes as the load unit (arbitrary units, AU). This is the Bangsbo sRPE method. Example: 60-minute session at RPE 7 = 420 AU.

## Linear Periodization

Progressive overload in a straight line: each week adds volume or intensity. Simple and effective for beginners. Drawback: plateaus quickly for advanced athletes.

## Undulating Periodization (DUP)

Vary training stimulus within the week. Example:

- Monday: Strength (5x5, 85% 1RM)
- Wednesday: Hypertrophy (3x12, 70% 1RM)
- Friday: Power (6x3, 75% 1RM)

## Block Periodization (for CrossFit)

3-4 week mesocycles focused on a specific quality:

1. Accumulation: high volume, moderate intensity (base building)
2. Transmutation: moderate volume, high intensity (specific preparation)
3. Realization: low volume, peak intensity (competition/testing week)
4. Deload: 40-60% volume reduction for supercompensation

## Deload Protocol

Every 3-4 weeks: reduce volume by 40-50%, maintain intensity. Signs you need a deload: persistent soreness, declining performance, elevated resting HR, poor sleep despite adequate duration.
