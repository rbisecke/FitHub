# CrossFit Movement Standards

## Air Squat

Standard: Full depth (hip crease below knee), full extension at top with knees tracking over toes. Feet shoulder-width apart, weight in heels. Common fault: knee cave (valgus collapse), remedy: cue knees out.

## Thruster

Front rack → squat below parallel → drive up → press to full lockout overhead in one continuous motion. Bar path must be vertical. Standards require full squat depth and arms locked out overhead simultaneously at top.

## Pull-up (Strict)

Dead hang start, chin clears bar at top. Hollow body position maintained. Kipping allowed in WODs unless specified strict. Band scaling options: light, medium, heavy band.

## Deadlift

Conventional stance, bar over mid-foot, neutral spine, drive through floor. Lockout requires hips and knees fully extended, shoulders behind bar. No hitching allowed in competition.

## Snatch

Hip-width stance, hook grip recommended. First pull: bar stays close, lats engaged. Second pull: explosive hip extension. Receive in overhead squat with active shoulder. Stabilize before standing.

## Clean and Jerk

Clean: hip-width → full front squat → stand. Jerk: dip-drive-split or push jerk. Arms locked out overhead, feet reunited before lowering. Full extension of arms and legs required at top.

## Kettlebell Swing (American)

Hike back, hip hinge, explosive hip snap, bell travels overhead. Arms straight at top, ears visible between arms. Glutes and core fully contracted. Standard for CrossFit: American swing (overhead).

## Handstand Push-up (Kipping)

Kick up to wall. Head touches pad/abmat, kip with hips, press to lockout. Standards: arms locked, body straight at top. Scaling: DB push press, pike push-up, box pike HSPU.

## Box Jump

Two-foot takeoff, land in full squat or stand to full extension at top. Step down preferred for high volume (tendon safety). Rebound jumping is advanced — requires Achilles health.

## Double-Under

Rope passes twice per jump. Rebound jump: stay on balls of feet, relax arms, wrists drive rope. Technique fault: "donkey kick" — cue straight body, small jump.
